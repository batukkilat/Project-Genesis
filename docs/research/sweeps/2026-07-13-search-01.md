# Search 01 — first run of the evolutionary loop

The first real `genesis search` run (Q-2026-07-13-C), probing the
chains/sieve neighborhood. Spec: [sweeps/search-01.ron](../../../sweeps/search-01.ron)
(seed 20260713, 5 generations × 8 children, survivors 3, σ 0.3, screen
3000 ticks / every 100, top-2 confirmation at 6000 ticks, bond cap
200 000). Raw artifacts — every individual's config/pack/ancestry/score,
`leaderboard.md`, `summary.ron` — live next to this doc in
`2026-07-13-search-01/`; the run is byte-reproducible from the spec.

Machine: 4-core Intel Xeon @ 2.80GHz (cloud box), build `414fc7e`
(rustc 1.96.1, release), `--threads 2` (sharing the box with the sieve
20k score run). Wall time: 1913.8 s for 42 evaluations + 2 confirmations.

## Outcome in one paragraph

The sieve lineage displaced chains immediately (fitness 75.7 vs 20.4 at
generation 0) and then climbed **one percent** in five generations:
champion `g005-i004` reached fitness 76.44 via an interpretable mutation
(the IMPRINT rule's self-gate rewired from energy to matter — imprinters
now qualify by mass, not by fuel). No discovered regime approaches the
corpus top (`actual`, headline 3631 at 20k ticks); the champion's capped
6k confirmation reached headline 426. The run's real product is four
design lessons about the instrument, recorded below.

## Findings

### 1. The sieve neighborhood is a fitness plateau (at this σ and horizon)

Every surviving mutant scores 75.3–76.4 — spread ≈ 1.5% — and the
champion beats the seed by 1%. Single σ=0.3 mutations move the regime
almost nowhere: the sieve dynamic (cull/split/imprint homeostasis) is
robust to small parameter changes, which is good news for sieve as
*content* (not a knife-edge regime) and bad news for local search as a
*discovery mechanism*. Escaping the plateau needs bolder moves — several
mutations per child, larger σ, cross-regime seeds — or a fitness signal
the plateau does not saturate (finding 2). Negative result worth keeping:
re-searching this neighborhood with the same settings is a dead region.

### 2. The screen horizon saturates the lifetime term

At 3000 ticks / 30 samples, *every* persistent regime maxes the lifetime
term (30/30 samples — the whole surviving population shows
`lifetime_peak = 30`), so `ln(1+lifetime_peak)` is a shared constant and
selection discriminates on structure count and information only. The
screen effectively runs a two-term fitness. Options for search-02, in
preference order: score lifetime as a *fraction of the horizon* with
sub-sample resolution (an Observer-side change — parked, per the
no-metric-bending rule, as a QUESTIONS.md candidate only if plateau
evidence persists), or simply screen longer and accept fewer evaluations.

### 3. A bond cap tuned to the screen caps every longer run

Bonds in sieve-class regimes grow roughly linearly with time (~150k at
3k ticks). The 200k cap — sized from the 3k-tick screen — therefore
tripped **both** 6k-tick confirmations (at ticks 4100 and 3900), which
was arithmetic, not bad luck. The records are still honest (capped runs
stamp the ticks actually simulated; both scored ~430 headline at capping)
and determinism is intact, but the confirmation stage produced no
uncapped long-horizon record. Lesson for the spec author: the cap is a
*per-evaluation cost bound*, so it must scale with the evaluation's
horizon (cap ≈ expected-bonds(horizon) × headroom), or confirmation
needs its own cap field. A search-02 spec should set
`bond_cap ≳ 500k` for 6k confirms of this regime class.

### 4. BIND is both the metric's visibility and the cost driver

The two structurally interesting mutation outcomes were both about the
bond rule: **drop BIND → fitness 0** (the regime may still have its
cull/split/imprint dynamics, but with no bonds it is invisible to every
v1 Observer metric — baseline finding 2 rediscovered by the search,
from the inside); **duplicate BIND → capped** (doubling the bond-creation
rate is instant runaway, g002-i007 at 307k bonds by 3k ticks). The search
lives exactly on the ridge between observer-invisible and
condensation-runaway, which is another way of saying the current metric
family measures bond structure and nothing else.

### 5. Corpus comparison remains open — and is expensive for this class

The exit criterion compares at the corpus horizon (20k ticks). A
sieve-class champion carries ~150k bonds at 3k ticks and grows; its 20k
evaluation is bands-class work (the shipped sieve 20k score run itself
exceeded a full shift on this box, still running as this doc lands).
Reproduce-when-a-box-is-free:

```sh
genesis score --config docs/research/sweeps/2026-07-13-search-01/g005/g005-i004.config.ron \
              --rules  docs/research/sweeps/2026-07-13-search-01/g005/g005-i004.pack.ron \
              --ticks 20000 --every 100 --out g005-i004.20k.score.ron
```

Until that runs, the honest statement is: **no discovered regime beats
the shipped corpus yet**, and the first search's contribution is
instrument knowledge, not a discovery.

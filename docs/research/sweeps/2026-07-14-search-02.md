# Search 02 — bolder steps out of the sieve plateau

The second `genesis search` run and the first under instrument v1.1
(Q-2026-07-14-A): a controlled comparison against search-01 on the same
ground. Spec: [sweeps/search-02.ron](../../../sweeps/search-02.ron) —
same seeds (chains, sieve), same screen horizon (3 000 ticks / every
100), same observer; only the step size (σ 0.6, `mutations_per_child` 3
vs σ 0.3 × 1) and the confirmation cap (`confirm_bond_cap` 600 000,
sized by search-01 finding 3's rule) differ. Budget: 62 individuals
over 6 generations, top 2 confirmed at 6 000 ticks.

Machine: 4-core Intel Xeon @ 2.80 GHz (cloud box), build `4e9a53d`
(rustc 1.96.1, release), all cores. Wall time: 4 041.4 s for 62
evaluations + 2 confirmations. Raw artifacts live next to this doc in
`2026-07-14-search-02/`; the run is byte-reproducible from the spec.

## Outcome in one paragraph

Bolder steps escaped the plateau — and left sieve entirely. The
champion `g006-i009` screens at fitness **79.13**, +4.5 % over the
sieve seed (75.70) where search-01's five generations managed +1 %, and
its 6 000-tick confirmation ran uncapped to fitness **91.42** / headline
588.15 — the instrument fix working (search-01 got zero uncapped
confirmations). The interesting part is *how*: the winning lineage
stripped the sieve pack down (dropped FUEL and SPLIT in generation 1,
SHED in generation 3) and then multiplied information-carrying
bond-creation rules — the discovered regime is not a tuned sieve but a
qualitatively different world (finding 2). Its sibling `g006-i000`,
0.17 % behind at the screen, detonates 900 ticks past the screen
horizon (finding 4). At the corpus horizon the champion scores 2 421.60
— third place, beating three of the five scored shipped packs but not
`actual` or `bands` — the first discovered regime competitive at 20 k,
with the exit criterion still open (finding 5).

## Findings

### 1. Compound steps escape what single steps cannot (the controlled result)

Same seeds, same horizon, same fitness — only the step size differs —
and the outcome moves from +1 % (search-01, flat plateau, all survivors
within 1.5 %) to +4.5 % with a structurally different champion. The
mechanism is visible in the ancestry: the decisive moves are *rule
drops and duplications chained in one child* (g001-i000 drops FUEL and
SPLIT in a single step; g003-i005 drops SHED, rewires CULL's gate, and
duplicates BIND in a single step). A single-mutation search tried each
of these moves alone (search-01 finding 4: drop BIND → fitness 0) and
found them individually lethal or neutral; three-op children can pay a
cost with one op and cash it with the next before selection judges the
sum. That is the textbook argument for compound mutation, now measured
on this instrument.

### 2. The search left sieve: the champion is an accretive imprint-web

The winning lineage's pack, by generation 6: CULL (absorb, now
energy-gated), IMPRINT (info_copy, now also leaking a little energy),
and **three BIND variants** — two of which transfer information along
the new bond — with no SPLIT (emit), no FUEL, and no SHED (bond_break).
No reproduction, no bond turnover: population only shrinks (9 477 of
10 000 left at 6 k), bonds only accrete, and information rides on
bond-creation itself. Structures are slowly growing webs (largest 58
members at 6 k) with **bounded, near-linear bond growth** — 63 k → 94 k
→ 286 k at 3 k / 6 k / 20 k, versus the seed sieve's 166 k already at
3 k and runaway past 20 k. Fitness
saw exactly what it was built to see: information (20 926 vs the seed's
7 326) and persistent structure at a fraction of the bond cost. But note
what was traded away: sieve's homeostasis (cull/split renewal) is gone;
this regime is a one-way accretion process, persistent but not
self-renewing. The observer's *possibly growing* hypothesis fires on it
at full confidence; nothing in the metric family can (yet) distinguish
"maintains itself" from "merely accumulates".

### 3. `confirm_bond_cap` did its job in both directions

The champion's confirmation ran its full 6 000 ticks uncapped under the
600 k cap (search-01: both confirmations spuriously killed by the
screen-sized cap) — finding 3's sizing rule (expected bonds at *that*
horizon × headroom) held: 94 k actual vs 600 k cap. And the cap still
fired where it should: `g006-i000`'s confirmation hit 767 946 bonds at
tick 3 900. A cost bound that neither kills honest runs nor lets
runaways burn the box is exactly what the two-field design bought.

### 4. Near-ties at the screen are not near-ties in dynamics

`g006-i009` and `g006-i000` are siblings — children of `g005-i001`,
0.17 % apart in screen fitness, 62.8 k vs 62.7 k bonds at tick 3 000.
The champion's ops include dropping one of the parent's four BIND
variants; the sibling kept all four (its three ops are small jitters).
Past the screen horizon they diverge qualitatively: the champion's bond
count stays bounded through 20 k ticks (286 k), the sibling multiplies
its bond count **twelve-fold in 900 ticks** (62.7 k → 767.9 k by tick
3 900, largest structure 7 279 of 9 471 particles — deep condensation).
The screen cannot see a detonation scheduled just past its horizon, and
fitness cannot either: confirmation is not a formality but the only
stage that distinguishes these two. Practical consequence: `confirm_top`
should stay ≥ 2 — confirming only the #1 would have left the
detonation undiscovered and the "plateau escaped" claim unqualified.

### 5. The corpus still wins the exit criterion — but the gap is now measurable

At the 3 k gate (docs/research/sweeps/2026-07-14-shipped-packs-3k.md):
the champion beats every corpus row on **fitness** (79.13 vs sieve's
75.70) — the function the search climbs — but sits fourth on the
**exit-criterion scalar** (253.87 vs sandbox 369.81, sieve 318.55,
bands 291.47), *below its own seed*. That is fitness v1's saturating
design working as intended (it discounts the big-blob headline the
scalar rewards), and it means "search finds fitter worlds" and "search
finds corpus-beating headline scores" remain different claims. Unlike
sieve, though, this champion's bounded bond growth makes the 20 k
corpus-horizon evaluation *affordable* — it ran in this session
(record: `2026-07-14-search-02/g006-i009.20k.score.ron`):

**Champion at 20 k: headline 2 421.60 — third against the corpus' 20 k
table** (actual 3 631.36, bands 2 711.93 above it; sandbox 2 178.48,
full-stack 1 806.26, chains 1 196.38 below; sieve has no honest 20 k row
— runaway, finding 6 of the baseline sweep). The run cost 489 s — the
affordability claim held (bonds 286 k at 20 k; sieve-class runs carry
millions and take hours). Structures: 284 final (largest 168 members,
complexity peak 12.63), information 50 932, population drained to
9 469, every surviving web at full 200/200-sample lifetime. So the
first search-discovered regime now **beats three of the five scored
shipped packs at the corpus horizon**, 11 % short of bands and 33 %
short of actual — measured, reproducible, and cheap to iterate on. The
exit criterion (beat *every* shipped pack) remains open, and the gap
has a shape: the two packs still ahead buy their headline with
slow-condensing giants (largest 4 916 and 6 904 members), exactly the
regime class fitness v1 deliberately discounts. Whether a
non-condensing regime can reach 3 600+ on this scalar — or whether the
scalar itself structurally favors condensation — is now the sharpest
open question in Phase 6.5, and it is an *evidence-backed* one.

### 6. Cost profile of a v1.1 search

4 041 s for 62 evaluations + 2 confirmations on four cores (~65 s per
evaluation amortized) — the entire search cost about a third of the
single sieve 20 k score attempt this box cut off unfinished at 3 h 35 m.
Stripped-pack children are cheap (the champion class screens in ~30 s,
its 20 k evaluation in 489 s); the expense
concentrates in capped runaways, which the cap bounds. Searches of this
shape can run nightly.
